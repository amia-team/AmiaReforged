Overview

The Prayer System allows characters to pray to their chosen deity and receive temporary blessings. The system works differently depending on your character class, alignment, and chosen deity.

Key Features:
  • Deity-specific bonuses unique to each god
  • Class-based prayer mechanics (Clerics, Druids, Paladins, Rangers, etc.)
  • Party-wide blessings for devoted followers
  • Domain bonuses for Clerics

How to Pray

Via Dashboard: Use the Pray button in the player dashboard (rest menu)

You must have a valid, character-appropriate deity set in order to Pray and get bonuses. You can choose a deity by typing the name (as spelled on this page) into the deity field at character creation, or by finding the deity's idol in the game world.

You can change your deity at any time unless you are higher than character level 10. Changes after level 10 require a request. If you do not have a deity set, you can choose a deity for the first time whenever you want, regardless of your level.

When you pray, your character will perform a meditation animation for approximately 8 seconds while the prayer is processed.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: Prayer bonuses do not stack with multiple prayers. If a cleric prays, their more powerful prayer with domain bonuses will overwrite any other existing prayer effect. You can, however, overwrite your own prayer effect if you would prefer to have the deity bonuses from your own deity. Just do this after any other prayers have been applied. You will not overwrite the prayer on everyone else in your party by doing this.

Requirements to Pray

Basic Requirements

  1. Must have a deity — Characters without a deity receive: "The gods do not care for the Faithless..."
  2. Deity must exist in Amia — If no idol exists for the deity: "[Deity] holds no domain on Amia..."
  3. Must not be Fallen — Characters with the Fallen status cannot receive blessings and will be smited
⚠️ Warning: Divine casters (Clerics, Druids, Paladins, Rangers, Blackguards, Divine Champions) with incorrect alignment will become Fallen and lose their divine connection! They will also be smited if they attempt to pray despite being Fallen.

Class-Based Prayer Mechanics

⚔️ Clerics

Requirements:

  • Must have matching alignment with deity
  • Must have BOTH domains that match the deity's domains

Effects:

  • ✓ 100% Success Rate
  • ✓ Always Party-Wide (affects all party members in the same area)
  • ✓ Receives Deity-Specific Bonus
  • ✓ Receives BOTH Domain Bonuses
Failure: Wrong alignment → Becomes Fallen + Smited
Domains don't both match → Becomes Fallen + Smited

🌿 Druids

Requirements:

  • Must have matching alignment with deity
  • Deity must be a valid "druid god" (has the Animal, Plant, Moon, or Sun Domain, or is otherwise indicated through scripting)

Effects:

  • ✓ 100% Success Rate
  • ✓ Always Party-Wide
  • ✓ Receives Deity-Specific Bonus
  • ✗ Does NOT receive domain bonuses

🛡️ Paladins, Rangers, Blackguards, Divine Champions

Requirements:

  • Must have matching alignment with deity

Effects:

  • ✓ 100% Personal Success Rate
  • Party-Wide Chance: 40% + 2% per divine level (up to 100%)

Note: All levels you have in divine classes (including Cleric and Druid) count toward your Divine Level. Example: Paladin 10/Divine Champion 20 will have 30 Divine Levels and get a full 100% chance for a party-wide prayer bonus. Cleric 10/Ranger 5/Blackguard 15 will have 30 Divine Levels and get a full 100% chance for a party-wide prayer bonus.

Whichever class you have the most levels in (or the last one you took, if they have the same amount) will determine which prayer version you get. In the above examples, the first would be a Divine Champion prayer, while the second one would be a Blackguard prayer.

The Fallen State

Divine casters become Fallen when:

  1. Their alignment doesn't match the deity's accepted alignments
  2. (Clerics only) None of their domains match the deity's domains
Effects of Being Fallen:
  • Receives "The Fall" item in their inventory
  • Gets smited immediately (divine damage = current HP + 3)
  • Cannot receive any blessings until atonement (Personal quest overseen by a DM)

Smite Damage

When smited, the character takes divine damage equal to their current HP + 3.

This means you will have to stabilize to survive being smited by a god. (Regeneration is your friend.)

Duration = 300 seconds + (Divine Level × 20 seconds)

⚙️ Deity Bonus Scaling Reference

All deity bonuses marked with a 🔗 symbol follow consistent, predictable scaling based on your Divine Level (sum of all divine class levels: Cleric + Druid + Ranger + Paladin + Blackguard + Divine Champion). Use the sections below to determine exactly what each type of bonus will be at your level.

⚔️ Attack Bonus or Attack Bonus vs. Alignment/Race (🔗)

Formula: 1 + 1 per 4 Divine Levels, capped at +6

  • Divine Level 1-3: +1 to hit
  • Divine Level 4-7: +2 to hit
  • Divine Level 8-11: +3 to hit
  • Divine Level 12-15: +4 to hit
  • Divine Level 16-19: +5 to hit
  • Divine Level 20+: +6 to hit (capped)

Example: Angharradh with "+AB vs Drow" at Divine Level 10 = +3 to hit vs Drow enemies

🛡️ Armor Class vs. Alignment/Race (🔗)

Formula: 1 + 1 per 4 Divine Levels, capped at +6

  • Divine Level 1-3: +1 AC
  • Divine Level 4-7: +2 AC
  • Divine Level 8-11: +3 AC
  • Divine Level 12-15: +4 AC
  • Divine Level 16-19: +5 AC
  • Divine Level 20+: +6 AC (capped)

Example: "+AC vs Good" at Divine Level 7 = +2 AC against Good enemies

💪 Physical/Magic/Divine Damage Immunity % (🔗)

Formula: 1% + Divine Level / 7

  • Divine Level 1: 1%
  • Divine Level 7: 2%
  • Divine Level 14: 3%
  • Divine Level 21: 4%
  • Divine Level 28: 5%

Note: These always cap at 5% to keep it in line with our property point values, as they are quite valuable.

🔥 Elemental Damage Immunity % (🔗)

Formula: 3% + Divine Level / 4

  • Divine Level 1: 3%
  • Divine Level 4: 4%
  • Divine Level 8: 5%
  • Divine Level 12: 6%
  • Divine Level 20: 8%
  • Divine Level 28: 10%

Elements: Fire, Cold, Electrical, Acid, Sonic

🛡️ Damage Resistance (🔗)

Formula: 1/- + 1 per 5 Levels, capped at 5/-

  • Divine Level 1-5: 1/- resistance
  • Divine Level 6-10: 2/- resistance
  • Divine Level 11-15: 3/- resistance
  • Divine Level 16-20: 4/- resistance
  • Divine Level 21+: 5/- resistance (capped)

Example: "5/- Bludgeoning Resistance" at level 2 = 1/-, level 26 = 5/-

⚡ Damage Shield (🔗)

This is a Biteback feature.

Damage Formula: 1 + 1 per 10 Divine Levels (for all damage types)

  • Divine Level 1-9: 1 damage to enemies that hit you
  • Divine Level 10-19: 2 damage to enemies that hit you
  • Divine Level 20-29: 3 damage to enemies that hit you
  • Divine Level 30+: 4 damage to enemies that hit you

Example: "1 Slashing Damage Shield" becomes 2 Slashing Damage at level 10 and 3 Slashing Damage at level 20

💎 Damage Reduction (🔗)

Formula: 1/+1 per 4 Divine Levels, capped at 5/+5

  • Divine Level 1-3: 1/+1 DR
  • Divine Level 4-7: 2/+2 DR
  • Divine Level 8-11: 3/+3 DR
  • Divine Level 12-15: 4/+4 DR
  • Divine Level 16+: 5/+5 DR (capped)

Examples: Damage vs Evil, Damage vs Good, Damage vs Chaotic, Damage vs Lawful

🐉 Summons & Servants

Deity summons are attached to the praying character as henchmen. They vary in how they scale:

  • Geb and Gond: Creature Level and power scales with Divine Level (+1 creature level per Divine Level)
  • Grumbar and Velsharoon: Number and type of creatures scales with Divine Level.
    • Divine Level 1-9: 2 Small Creatures
    • Divine Level 10-16: 3 Medium Creatures
    • Divine Level 17-30: 4 Large Creatures
📋 Divine Level Reference: Divine Level = sum of: Cleric + Druid + Ranger + Paladin + Blackguard + Divine Champion levels

📝 Notes

  • Conditional Bonuses: Some deity bonuses are conditional (e.g., night-only bonuses for Baravar Cloakshadow, Eilistraee, and Selûne; indoor bonus for Ibrandul).
  • Divine Level: Calculated as the sum of all divine class levels (Cleric + Druid + Ranger + Paladin + Blackguard + Divine Champion).
  • "Versus" Bonuses: Effects tagged vs. specific alignments or races may not function against all intended targets due to engine limitations.
